About

Science Goals and Objectives of EMIT To acquire remote-sensing measurements of the abundance of surface minerals — namely, hematite, goethite, illite, vermiculite, calcite, dolomite, montmorillonite, kaolinite, chlorite, and gypsum — in arid regions between 50-degree south and north latitudes in Africa, Asia, North America, South America, and Australia To deliver an improved assessment of the heating and cooling effects of mineral dust in Earth’s atmosphere To improve predictions of how future climate scenarios

Climate Change Models

we can integrate EMIT data into climate change models. This makes future climate change predictions more precise and can help us better prepare for these changes.

Combat Agains Global Warming

EMIT sensors can track its sources and movements. This assists us in developing strategies to combat global warming.
